Chicken Biryani

Ingredients Required and Instructions

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless chicken, cut into small pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 3 onions, sliced thinly
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves and fried onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice in cold water until the water runs clear, then soak the rice in cold water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, bring 6-7 cups of water to a boil. Add salt, bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon stick to the water.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 80% done, then strain the rice and keep it aside.
  4. In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove the onions and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, add the chicken and fry until it is cooked through.
  6. Add ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt to the chicken. Stir well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add yogurt to the chicken and stir until it is well combined. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until the chicken is tender and the gravy has thickened.
  8. In a large pot, layer the cooked rice and chicken gravy, starting with a layer of rice at the bottom of the pot. Repeat until all the rice and chicken is used up.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  10. Once done, remove the lid and garnish with fried onions and fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot.

Tips

  • Use good quality basmati rice for best results.
  • Soaking the rice before cooking helps to make it fluffy and separate.
  • Don't overcook the rice as it will become mushy and lose its texture.
  • Use boneless chicken for easier cooking and eating, but you can also use bone-in chicken if you prefer.
  • To make the biryani more flavorful, you can also add saffron to the rice while cooking.
